Top To The Rich-Initial

"Top to the Rich: A Journey from Rags to Riches" debuted in 2023, offering an immersive gaming experience for both web and Windows platforms. Developed on GODOT 3.5, this work-in-progress game puts players in the shoes of a struggling individual striving to overcome poverty.

In "Top to the Rich" ,players undertake a variety of tasks—from heart-pounding races to daring heists—to earn money and climb the economic ladder. Whether investing in education to acquire new skills or purchasing weapons from the local gun shop, every decision counts on the path to prosperity.

Currently, the game offers the chase mode, with more features in development. Available for installation or instant play on itch.io, "Top to the Rich" promises an engaging experience that will keep players coming back for more updates and challenges. Join the journey from rags to riches and test your skills in this exciting adventure.

Nightmare: Pumpkin Slayer | GameJam

"Nightmare: Pumpkin Slayer" marks my second game, crafted during the Mini Jam 144: Nightmare. Placing Overall Rank #23 with a score of 3.019 the challenge was to develop a game within three days, starting from October 27th, 2023, at 9:45 AM to October 30th, 2023, at 9:45 AM. The jam's theme was Nightmare, with the limitation of a Colossal Enemy.

In this platformer survival game, players face off against colossal pumpkin enemies. Each attack is lethal to these foes but drains the player's sleep meter. To replenish health and sleep, players must find and rest in beds scattered throughout the game world. It's a thrilling race against time and exhaustion in this nightmarish adventure.

Numerical Computation : Bisection Method

The bisection method is a simple yet powerful numerical technique used for finding roots of equations. It operates by repeatedly dividing the interval containing the root in half and selecting the subinterval where the function changes sign, effectively narrowing down the possible root location with each iteration. By iteratively refining the interval, the method converges to the root with a guaranteed level of accuracy. Its simplicity and reliability make it a popular choice for solving equations in various fields, including mathematics, engineering, and computer science.
